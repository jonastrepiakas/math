\title{Geometric Representations of the Braid Group}

\author{Jonas Trepiakas}



\begin{document}

\begin{abstract}
    \noindent
    The mapping class group of a surface $S$ is the
    group of isotopy classes of automorphisms of $S$. More
    precisely, it is defined
    as $\MCG(S) :=
    \pi_0 \left( \Homeo^{+}\left( S, \partial S \right)  \right) $ 
    where $\Homeo^{+}\left( S, \partial \right) $ is the group
    of self-homeomorphisms of $S$ fixing the boundary pointwise,
    equipped with a suitable topology. 

    \noindent We will look at certain connections between mapping class groups
    and braid groups. The braid group on $n$ strands,
    $\mathcal{B}_n$,
    is the group of isotopy classes of braids on $n$ strands.
    See figure \ref{fig:braid-png}.\\


    \noindent The goal of the
    lecture will be to understand the 
    basic structure of these groups as well
    as geometric representations of the braid
    group, which
    are group homomorphisms $\mathcal{B}_n
    \to \MCG(S)$ for some surface $S$.\\

    \noindent We will also see how geometric representations fit
    into a categorical framework 
    which turns out to be the same one
    needed in the study of homological stability. \\

    \noindent There are no prerequisites for most of this talk as the
subject will be treated informally.
\end{abstract}

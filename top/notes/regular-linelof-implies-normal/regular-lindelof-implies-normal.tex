    \begin{proof}
        Take two closed disjoint subsets $A,B \subset X$ 
        and using regularity,
        find an open cover for A by sets whose closure lies in
        $X - B$ and similarly an open cover for  $B$ by sets whose
        closure lies in $X-A$.\\
        Extract disjoint subcovers from this using a trick.\\
        \linebreak
        \textit{Hint:} See the proof of theorem 32.1 in Munkres.
    \end{proof}

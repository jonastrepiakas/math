 (d) Suppose $X \times Y = A \cup B$. Let $X_A = 
    \left\{ p \in X  \colon p \times Y \subset A \right\} $ and
    $X_B = \left\{ p \in X  \colon p \times Y \subset B \right\} $.
    Considering the sets in the Zariski topology, we see that
    \[
    p \cross Y = \left( p \cross Y \cap A \right) \cup \left( p \cross Y \cap B \right) 
    \] 
    and since each $p \cross Y$ is irreducible as it is isomorphic to $Y$, each
    $p \cross Y$ is contained in $A$ or $B$. Hence $X = A \cup B$

    \begin{exercise}[9.16]
        Let $\left[ A \right] $ be a real
        $n \times n$ matrix. Show for its complex Jordan normal
        form that every Jordan block with a nonreal eigenvalue can be paired
        with a Jordan block of the same size and the conjugated eigenvalue.
    \end{exercise}


    \begin{solution}
        Suppose
        $\left[ A |_{M_i} \right] $ is a Jordan block
        occuring in some normal form of $A$ over
        $\mathbb{C}$. Let $\lambda_i$ be the 
        eigenvalue. Then by assumption,
        letting $M_i$ denote the generalized eigenspace for
        the eigenvalue $\lambda_i$, we get
        $M_i =
        \left\{ x \in V  \mid 
        \exists k > 0 \colon 
    \left( A - \lambda \right)^{k} x = 0 \right\} $.
    Now, taking the complex conjugate over this equation (considered
    as matrices such that $\left[ A \right] $ is
    real), we get
    \[
        \left( \left[ A \right] - \overline{\lambda} 
        I \right)^{k} \overline{x} = 0
    \]  
    so
    $\overline{x} \in 
    M_{\overline{\lambda_i}}$, hence
    $\overline{\lambda_i}$ must be some $\lambda_j$. And
    clearly the above argument gives
    $\dim M_{i} = \dim M_{j}$, the bijective correspondence
    being $x \mapsto \overline{x}$.
    \end{solution}

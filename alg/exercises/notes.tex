\begin{exercise}[13.4.(5)]
    Let $K$ be a finite extension of $F$. Prove that
    $K$ is a splitting field over $F$ if and only if
    every irreducible polynomial in $F[x]$ that has
    a root in $K$ splits completely in $K[x]$.
\end{exercise}

\begin{proof}
    Suppose $\left\{ f_i \right\} \subset F[x]$ is the
    collection of polynomials over $F$ that split over
    $K$. Suppose $g \in F[x]$ is irreducible and has
    a root $\alpha \in K$. We must show that
    $g \in \left\{ f_i \right\} $.
    Since $K$ is a finite extension, we have
    $K = F\left( \alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_n \right) $ for
    $\alpha_i$ algebraic over $F$.
    Let $K'$ be a splitting field for
    $\left\{ f_i \right\} \cup \left\{ g \right\} $ over
    $F$ which we can obtain by
    adjoining roots of $g'$ to $K$ if we write
    $g (x) = (x-k) g'(x)$ for $k \in K$.
    Suppose we adjoin the roots
    $\beta_1, \ldots, \beta_m$.
\end{proof}
